#+TITLE: Evergreen Notes
#+DATE: 2025-02-26
# %%% haystack-end-frontmatter %%%
Evergreen notes are a knowledge-management practice articulated by Andy
Matuschak: notes written to be refined and updated over time rather than left as
static records. Unlike fleeting notes or literature summaries, an evergreen note
captures your current best thinking on a concept and is revisited, edited, and
strengthened as your understanding evolves. The "evergreen" metaphor emphasizes
durability: these zettel are not dated diary entries but living documents that
accumulate precision and nuance through repeated engagement. Evergreen notes
follow the atomicity principle — each note should be about one idea, expressed
clearly enough to stand alone without the context that surrounded its creation.
Titles matter enormously for evergreen notes: a title like "Concept-oriented
titles make notes more useful" communicates a full claim, unlike a topic label
like "Titles" which is just a category. In an Emacs-based pkm workflow,
evergreen notes are typically org files with a stable identifier in the
filename; Haystack's frecency engine makes it easy to replay the search chains that
surface evergreen notes, keeping familiar search paths within easy reach. The practice of writing evergreen notes is closely related to
the zettelkasten method but extends to any second-brain system: the value comes
from investing in quality notes that compound over time. Reviewing and updating
evergreen notes is as important as creating them — a note that was accurate two
years ago may now need revision, and the act of revision deepens understanding.
Linking evergreen notes to each other, either through org-roam's backlink system
or through Haystack's full-text search retrieval, is what transforms isolated
notes into a network of ideas. Building a corpus of well-maintained evergreen
notes is one of the highest-return investments in a personal knowledge-
management practice.
