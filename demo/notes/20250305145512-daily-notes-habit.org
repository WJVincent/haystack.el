#+TITLE: Daily Notes Habit
#+DATE: 2025-03-05
# %%% haystack-end-frontmatter %%%
The daily note is a capture mechanism used in many personal knowledge management
(pkm) systems: a date-stamped note that serves as the entry point for everything
captured that day. Daily notes lower the barrier for capture — instead of
deciding where a new idea belongs in your second-brain, you write it in today's
note and process it later when you have time to think. The daily note is
inherently temporary: its primary purpose is to be processed, with each captured
item either discarded, elaborated into a permanent zettel, or linked to an
existing note. Org mode's capture templates with a date-based file target are a
natural implementation: ~C-c c d~ drops you into a dated daily note without
interrupting your current task. Logseq and Obsidian both feature daily notes
prominently as their primary entry interface, reflecting how the daily note
habit has become central to modern pkm culture. The risk of daily notes is that
they become a graveyard of unprocessed captures: items logged but never
reviewed, creating the illusion of a productive knowledge-management practice
without the substance. A weekly review habit paired with daily notes is the
standard remedy: once a week, process unreviewed daily note items into permanent
notes, update evergreen notes, and clear the capture queue. In Haystack, daily
notes are just files in the notes directory like any other; their date-based
filenames ensure they sort chronologically and the frecency engine naturally de-
emphasizes old unvisited daily notes. The daily note habit is most valuable as a
friction-reducer at the capture stage — it should not become an end in itself
but a means of feeding a zettelkasten or evergreen notes corpus. Some
practitioners skip daily notes entirely in favor of direct capture to permanent
notes; the right choice depends on whether you find the daily note's low-
friction inbox useful or just a deferred inbox.
