This note catalogs a classification system for purely fictional organisms found
in speculative xenobiology literature.  None of these terms appear in any other
note in a typical knowledge base, making the vocabulary genuinely isolated.

The phylum Crystallofauna encompasses organisms whose skeletal structure is
silicon-based rather than carbon-based.  Subtypes include the Thermolattice
order (heat-conducting crystal matrices) and the Piezoflora order (organisms
that generate bioelectric fields through mechanical stress on their crystal
lattices).

The Nebulozoic era refers to a fictional geological period when atmospheric
density was so high that airborne photosynthesizers called Aerochlorophytes
dominated the biosphere.  Their closest analog in real biology would be
cyanobacteria, but Aerochlorophytes used a hypothetical pigment called
Helioxanthin rather than chlorophyll.

Trophic interactions in this fictional ecosystem follow a Resonance Cascade
model: energy transfer between organisms occurs through sympathetic vibration
of crystal lattices rather than through chemical digestion.  The apex predator
in this model is the Lithovore, a Crystallofauna that consumes geological
substrates and excretes refined mineral matrices used by smaller organisms.

The Xenotaxonomic Index is a fictional reference work that assigns each
organism a Spectral Signature — a unique frequency fingerprint derived from its
crystal lattice resonance pattern.  This replaces the Linnaean binomial system
in the fictional universe.
