#+TITLE: Capture Workflow
#+DATE: 2025-04-30
# %%% haystack-end-frontmatter %%%
Capture is the first step in any knowledge-management (pkm) workflow: getting an
idea, observation, or reference into your system before it disappears from
working memory. The cardinal rule of capture is low friction — the harder it is
to capture, the more ideas you will lose to inertia, and the more valuable the
ones you do capture feel (a survivorship bias that distorts your sense of what
is worth writing down). Org mode's capture system (~org-capture~) is one of the
most powerful capture implementations: configurable templates can route captures
to different files (inbox, daily note, specific project) based on the template
chosen. Haystack's ~haystack-new-note~ command is designed for fast note
creation: it prompts for a title, generates a timestamped filename, inserts the
appropriate frontmatter, and opens the buffer for immediate writing. A capture
workflow should have at most two stages: capture (write it down now) and process
(decide what it means and where it belongs, later). Interrupting the capture
stage with filing decisions defeats the purpose: the goal is to capture without
breaking flow, not to maintain a perfectly organized system in real time.
Fleeting notes, literature notes, and permanent notes are three distinct capture
types in the zettelkasten model; each has different processing requirements and
a different destination in the second-brain. Mobile capture — capturing ideas
when away from your primary machine — requires a pipeline back to your main
notes directory; common solutions include a simple text file synced via cloud
storage or a dedicated mobile app with org-protocol integration. The capture-to-
review ratio matters: a healthy workflow processes captures promptly enough that
the inbox does not become an anxiety-inducing backlog. Reviewing captured notes
while they are still fresh — within a day or two — dramatically increases the
probability that they will be developed into evergreen notes rather than
forgotten in an inbox.
