been thinking about why I keep coming back to emacs after trying other
editors. it's not the keybindings, those are objectively weird. it's
not the aesthetics, VS Code looks better out of the box. it's the fact
that everything is a buffer. email is a buffer. git is a buffer. your
shell is a buffer. your notes are a buffer. and every buffer responds
to the same operations: search, narrow, act.

haystack leans into this. a search result is a buffer. a filter is a
buffer. the tree view is a buffer. you don't leave emacs to manage
your knowledge, you just open more buffers.

this is either profound or circular. maybe both. the medium is the
message and the message is "everything is text and text is searchable."

marshall mcluhan would have liked emacs. or hated it. hard to say.
