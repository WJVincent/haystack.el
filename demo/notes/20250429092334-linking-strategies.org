#+TITLE: Linking Strategies in PKM
#+DATE: 2025-04-29
# %%% haystack-end-frontmatter %%%
Linking is the mechanism that transforms a collection of notes (zettel) into a
knowledge network: each link declares a meaningful relationship between two
ideas. Not all links are equal in value: a link from a synthesis note to two
source zettel carries more intellectual weight than a tag-style link from a
topic index to a long list of tangentially related notes. The zettelkasten
tradition distinguishes between structural links (navigational, connecting notes
in a sequence or hierarchy), content links (semantic, connecting notes by shared
ideas), and tag links (categorical, grouping by type). In org-roam, links are
created with ~org-roam-insert~ and stored in a SQLite database, enabling
backlink queries that show all notes pointing to the current one. Haystack
relies on search-based implicit linking rather than explicit link maintenance:
two notes are "linked" whenever they share concepts that rg retrieval can
surface together. The overhead of explicit link creation is non-trivial:
choosing to link requires recognizing a relationship, deciding it is worth
recording, and creating the link without breaking your writing flow. Bi-
directional links (where both notes display the connection in a backlinks panel)
reduce the cost of linking by letting you create a one-directional link and have
the system infer the other direction. Over-linking is a real failure mode: notes
connected to hundreds of others via shallow links lose their identity and the
links lose their meaning, degrading into a flat tag cloud. A practical linking
heuristic: link when you actively want to be able to navigate from one note to
another, not just because two notes mention related topics. Combining explicit
links for key relationships with Haystack's search-based retrieval for broader
exploration gives the benefits of both approaches without the full maintenance
burden of either.
